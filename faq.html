Frequently Asked Questions — Generative AI

Generative AI (GenAI) refers to models and systems that can create new content such as text, images, audio, or code that resembles human-created content. Examples include large language models and image generators.

GenAI models are trained on large datasets to learn patterns in language, images, or other media. During training they adjust internal parameters to predict or generate realistic outputs. At inference time they use learned patterns to produce new content based on prompts.

Examples: Text: Chat-based LLMs (chat assistants), story writers. Images: image generators, e.g., text-to-image tools. Audio: tools for music and voice synthesis. Code: AI-assisted coding tools. (Specific brand names are frequently changing; for coursework, list examples you actually used.)

It can Generate drafts essays, summaries, brainstorm ideas, produce images and mockups, help debug or suggest code snippets, and speed up repetitive tasks. Great for prototyping, learning, and creative exploration.

Risks include hallucinations,confident but wrong answers, biased outputs reflecting training data, privacy issues, and misuse,plagiarism, and misinformation. Models can also fail at specialized reasoning requiring up-to-date facts.

Use GenAI as a learning aid: generate drafts, ask for explanations, and check your work. Always verify facts, cite AI where required, and avoid submitting AI-generated text as your own without permission.
