Analogy: Choosing a Wedding Documentation Team

A practical reflection comparing a wedding documentation team to AI-assisted developers.

Scenario

You are organizing your wedding. A documentation team offers ₱40,000 for a package. Their tools and profile:

  • Equipment: iPhone 16 Pro Max
  • Software: CapCut for same-day edit
  • Experience: 2 years
  • Portfolio: Above-average

Would you accept the deal?

Short answer: Yes — provisionally, after reviewing sample output and confirming expectations.

Why?

  1. Tool capability: Modern smartphones like the iPhone 16 Pro Max produce high-quality footage. Equipment alone doesn't guarantee results — the operator's skill matters most.
  2. Editing software: CapCut is capable of fast, polished edits when used by someone who knows pacing and color grading.
  3. Experience & portfolio: Two years plus an above-average portfolio suggests they have practical event experience and aesthetic sense.
  4. Value for money: ₱40,000 is reasonable for a same-day edit service with professional delivery, depending on deliverables (length of highlight video, raw files, rights).

Integrity of their work

Integrity is shown through transparent communication (what is included), clear usage of music licensing, delivery timelines, and ownership rights. Request sample edits from their portfolio that closely match the 'vibe' you want.

Reflection for "vibe coders" & AI-assisted devs

Just like the documentation team, developers who use AI tools should be judged by:

  • Output quality — clean, maintainable code and correct behavior
  • Creativity — problem-solving beyond what auto-generated code offers
  • Integrity — not passing AI output as one’s own without scrutiny
  • Skill level — understanding systems and architecture, not only APIs

Final recommendations before hiring:

  • Ask for a short sample or a small paid test
  • Clarify deliverables, turnaround, and licensing
  • Set acceptance criteria (e.g., color grade, audio mix level)
