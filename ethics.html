Ethical Use of Artificial Intelligence

Responsible use in the academe

  • Be transparent: disclose when AI contributed to your work.
  • Follow your institution's policy: Ask teachers about acceptable uses.
  • Use AI for learning, not to cheat: Use drafts to improve your understanding.

Avoiding plagiarism and misuse

Always rework AI outputs to reflect your thinking. If the assignment requires original work, AI-generated answers should not be submitted as-is.

Accuracy and verification

Double check facts, especially dates, citations and domain specific claims. Use reliable sources to collaborate AI outputs.

Bias and fairness

Understand that models reflect biases in training data. When using AI for sensitive topics, seek multiple perspectives and perform fairness checks.

Recommended guidelines

  1. Treat AI as an assistant — the human is responsible for final content and decisions.
  2. Document when AI was used and how it influenced outcomes.
  3. Cross-check facts and provide citations.
  4. Respect privacy and copyright — do not feed private or copyrighted data into public models without permission.
  5. Encourage inclusive and fair datasets where possible.

Quick ethics checklist

  • Did I verify the facts?
  • Did I credit AI assistance where required?
  • Could this output harm someone if published?
  • Am I respecting privacy and IP?
